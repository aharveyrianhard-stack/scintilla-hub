▸ How this works — model spec, wiring & sources (read me)

What it is. A live, connected equity workbench on the FMP API. The seven DCF sliders (growth, EBITDA margin, tax, risk-free, beta, MRP, perpetuity g) are the single source of truth — everything else (formulas, DCF, value split, sensitivity, reverse-DCF) recomputes together on every change.

The math. CoE = rᶠ + (β×MRP) · WACC = (wₑ·CoE)+(w_d·k_d·(1−t)) · UFCF = EBIT(1−t)+D&A−capex−ΔNWC · TV = FCF₅(1+g)/(WACC−g) · Fair value = (ΣPV(FCF)+PV(TV)−net debt)/shares. Base: FY26 rev $215.9B, 24.51B shares, net debt $0.8B; D&A 3.06%, capex 3.49%, NWC 22.7% of revenue.

Wiring rules. (1) Modes/chips are quick-sets of a slider — they highlight only when the slider matches, never hold a hidden value. (2) Tabs & default axes order by impact ranking everywhere. (3) Every data table freezes its top row + left column (Excel-style). (4) Each "read" feeds the step below it (comps growth band → reverse-DCF reasonableness; beta peer context → beta verdict).

Sources. Comps metrics-ratios-ttm + screener · DCF mechanics custom-dcf-advanced · forward estimates financial-estimates (analyst consensus) · insider insiderTrades · congress senate/house · COT commitmentOfTraders. Served from git → auto-deploy; durable URL survives hub deploys.

How to use. Set a scenario → pick beta/MRP methods (watch CAPM/WACC recompute) → read the value-split bar (how much rides on the perpetuity) → check the projected-path CAGR & source tab → use the tornado to find what matters, then the 2-var matrix on those two → flip to Reverse-DCF to see what the price assumes and whether it's reasonable vs the modes & peer betas.

Roadmap: ticker-driven at top · §3.5 consensus fully wired into DCF · range-width as confidence score · reverse-DCF backtest · insider/congress/COT signal views.

Methodology — where these numbers come from. The DCF's free cash flow is built, not pulled: FMP's custom-dcf-advanced projects revenue at your growth rate, applies your EBITDA margin, then subtracts D&A, capex (3.49% of revenue) and the change in working capital (22.7% of revenue) to get unlevered FCF — so operating margin and capex are assumptions, not analyst figures. Separately, the analyst consensus in §3.5 (financial-estimates) gives revenue, EBITDA, EBIT, SG&A, net income and EPS with low/avg/high and analyst counts — but NOT capex or operating cash flow, which is why a "pure analyst FCF" still needs a capex bridge from history. Who's behind the estimates: NVDA's near-year lines carry 30–40 analysts (KeyBanc, Needham, UBS, BofA, Evercore, RBC, Morgan Stanley-tier houses); coverage thins to ~7 by FY2031. See §3.5 for the full count-by-year.

3.5 · Analyst Consensus vs FMP Extrapolation — Visual Guide (not wired into the model)

endpoint: financial-estimates (included in your subscription — consensus, NOT TipRanks) · annual shown; quarterly also available (period=quarter)

Years across, line items down — the standard layout. Every period FMP's financial-estimates has for NVDA: 4 historical consensus years (what the Street expected then) + 6 forward years + two deliberately BLANK columns where coverage dies. Analyst counts sit at the top. Each cell = avg (low–high), $B except EPS. Gold row = FMP's DCF extrapolation for contrast: by FY2031 it sits at 2.7× the Street's own number.

🔍 The cliff is the point: counts climb into the near years (40 analysts on FY28 revenue), collapse going out (7 on FY31 EPS), and hit ZERO at FY32 — the blank columns are real, not decoration. FY22–FY25 are historical consensus snapshots (what the Street expected then — beat/miss checks come from comparing them to actuals). FMP publishes counts for revenue and EPS only; middle lines ride between those two. Quarterly (period=quarter): same six lines, ~10 forward quarters, 11–12 analysts each.
📊 Line-item depth strategy (per Alan): use the furthest-down line the estimates support, fall back up the statement as coverage thins. For NVDA all five lines exist every year, but the CONFIDENCE thins — EPS analysts drop 30→7 by FY2031 while revenue keeps 16. A consensus-based FCF proxy = EBITDA − capex − cash taxes; capex has no consensus line here, so it gets bridged from historicals (that's the "gap fill from us"). Quarterly estimates exist for near periods; annual carries the long horizon. Agreed base methodology: take the consensus TOP LINE (revenue — the most analysts, the longest horizon) and CALCULATE the bottom line ourselves via margins — using deeper consensus lines (EBITDA, net income) as cross-checks where coverage is thick enough. Paid estimate feeds (Zacks/Estimize, low hundreds/mo) logged for later — not now.
🟢 Free Buy-gauge + revision momentum (no TipRanks needed): grades-summary returns the live mix — NVDA today: 2 Strong Buy / 58 Buy / 16 Hold / 3 Sell → consensus "Buy" — and historical-ratings gives the same mix back in time = whether the Street is drifting toward or away from Buy. That IS the "Wall Street sentiment" TipRanks markets.
