Methodology вҖ” where these numbers come from. The DCF's free cash flow is built, not pulled: FMP's custom-dcf-advanced projects revenue at your growth rate, applies your EBITDA margin, then subtracts D&A, capex (3.49% of revenue) and the change in working capital (22.7% of revenue) to get unlevered FCF вҖ” so operating margin and capex are assumptions, not analyst figures. Separately, the analyst consensus in В§3.5 (financial-estimates) gives revenue, EBITDA, EBIT, SG&A, net income and EPS with low/avg/high and analyst counts вҖ” but NOT capex or operating cash flow, which is why a "pure analyst FCF" still needs a capex bridge from history. Who's behind the estimates: NVDA's near-year lines carry 30вҖ“40 analysts (KeyBanc, Needham, UBS, BofA, Evercore, RBC, Morgan Stanley-tier houses); coverage thins to ~7 by FY2031. See В§3.5 for the full count-by-year.

endpoint: financial-estimates (included in your subscription вҖ” consensus, NOT TipRanks) В· annual shown; quarterly also available (period=quarter)

Years across, line items down вҖ” the standard layout. Every period FMP's financial-estimates has for NVDA: 4 historical consensus years (what the Street expected then) + 6 forward years + two deliberately BLANK columns where coverage dies. Analyst counts sit at the top. Each cell = avg (lowвҖ“high), $B except EPS. Gold row = FMP's DCF extrapolation for contrast: by FY2031 it sits at 2.7Г— the Street's own number.

рҹ”Қ The cliff is the point: counts climb into the near years (40 analysts on FY28 revenue), collapse going out (7 on FY31 EPS), and hit ZERO at FY32 вҖ” the blank columns are real, not decoration. FY22вҖ“FY25 are historical consensus snapshots (what the Street expected then вҖ” beat/miss checks come from comparing them to actuals). FMP publishes counts for revenue and EPS only; middle lines ride between those two. Quarterly (period=quarter): same six lines, ~10 forward quarters, 11вҖ“12 analysts each.
рҹ“Ҡ Line-item depth strategy (per Alan): use the furthest-down line the estimates support, fall back up the statement as coverage thins. For NVDA all five lines exist every year, but the CONFIDENCE thins вҖ” EPS analysts drop 30вҶ’7 by FY2031 while revenue keeps 16. A consensus-based FCF proxy = EBITDA вҲ’ capex вҲ’ cash taxes; capex has no consensus line here, so it gets bridged from historicals (that's the "gap fill from us"). Quarterly estimates exist for near periods; annual carries the long horizon. Agreed base methodology: take the consensus TOP LINE (revenue вҖ” the most analysts, the longest horizon) and CALCULATE the bottom line ourselves via margins вҖ” using deeper consensus lines (EBITDA, net income) as cross-checks where coverage is thick enough. Paid estimate feeds (Zacks/Estimize, low hundreds/mo) logged for later вҖ” not now.
рҹҹў Free Buy-gauge + revision momentum (no TipRanks needed): grades-summary returns the live mix вҖ” NVDA today: 2 Strong Buy / 58 Buy / 16 Hold / 3 Sell вҶ’ consensus "Buy" вҖ” and historical-ratings gives the same mix back in time = whether the Street is drifting toward or away from Buy. That IS the "Wall Street sentiment" TipRanks markets.
